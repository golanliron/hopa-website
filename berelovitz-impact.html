Im Kalvia Emergency Fund
Patricio Paoletti Foundation
Sheba Hospital & Dizengoff Shelter · NIS 5,000
The grant helped fund the Healing Farm's activities at Sheba Hospital and the Dizengoff municipal shelter. Touch therapists including Shiatsu, reflexology and mental health professionals treated staff at Sheba in one-on-one 20-minute sessions. A mindfulness workshop with headphones was also held in rotating groups for staff. At the same time, touch therapy was provided at the Dizengoff shelter for residents who had fled there.
Thanks to the Berelowitz Foundation's support in this emergency, our Healing Farm team was able to provide meaningful treatment to Sheba's medical staff alongside Dizengoff Center residents. The therapeutic framework provided to staff during the war expanded and became a recurring program funded by the hospital itself for Sheba's emergency department staff. We are proud and happy to provide meaningful service thanks to support like this.

Student quotes from surveys: "The workshop opens people's hearts and makes them say their opinions and talk more than they usually do" (11th grade student, Carmiel). Or Simcha Children's Home
Remedial Education for At-Risk Children · NIS 40,000
Remedial teaching for at-risk children living in a therapeutic boarding home, who arrived with significant learning gaps and sometimes with no educational background at all. Lessons were given twice a week in small groups of two, focusing on reading, writing and spelling. Students showed an average 50% improvement in academic skills.
This project was exceptionally meaningful, especially given the challenging period Israel went through — a time of uncertainty, tensions and heavy emotional pressure that intensified the educational and emotional gaps among at-risk children. For the children of Or Simcha, who already grew up without stable family support, the continuity and stability the project provided within the storm were of immense value beyond the academic metrics alone.

Grant Report 2025
SAHAR — Online Crisis Response
Training Volunteers for Proactive Mental Health Outreach · NIS 50,000
Over three and a half months we trained 17 new volunteers for the "SAHAR Patrol" which identifies posts of severe mental distress on social media and forums, and proactively reaches out to the authors. In 2025 the patrol responded to 11,829 severe distress posts — a 75% increase from 2024. Approximately 118,290 young people ages 10-30 were exposed to SAHAR's posts. The patrol reported 333 cases of self-harm, contributing to actual life-saving.
The patrol has been operating since 2008 and to date has responded to the distress of hundreds of thousands of youth. The grant was extremely significant for expanding the patrol's activities, enabling us to strengthen proactive response precisely at a time when the need is growing. From the field we clearly see that young people are dealing with ongoing mental fatigue, alongside deep disruption of daily life frameworks. In such reality, the patrol has a critical role: not to wait for people in distress to reach out themselves, but to identify signs of distress in time and provide assistance proactively. We wish to thank the Berelowitz Family Foundation for the generous grant.

Alumah (Osot Chayil)
"Rosh Hashana Together" for Ultra-Orthodox Military Families · NIS 25,000
The initiative provides real respite for families of ultra-orthodox soldiers, both regular and reserve duty. It especially strengthens the women and children, relieving emotional and practical burden during a loaded and complex period in the ultra-orthodox calendar. About 500 participants (120 women plus children) from across the country.
The donation was relatively modest but vital. Without it, we would not have been able to realize the significant participation of the Ministry of Defense. 92% reported a sense of belonging with other ultra-orthodox families facing the same challenges. 75% reported a decrease in their children's distress. 32% of women introduced themselves through their family member's military unit — indicating a fundamental shift in perceptions about military service.

Planetherapy
International Resilience Center — Online Mental Health · NIS 210,000
An international resilience center providing online emotional support for Israelis and Jews, based on a network of professional therapists volunteering from around the world. About 63% of those who reached out chose to continue beyond the first session. ~40 new referrals per month. ~46% from communities outside Israel. ~88% of therapists specialize in mental health and trauma. ~45% provide multilingual support.
Planetherapy's impact is expressed not only at the individual level, but also in creating new infrastructure: a global, accessible and multilingual system capable of responding to distress in real time and continuing to accompany over time. The activity began as an emergency response, but the data points to an ongoing need.
